Our Story

NWA Second Stage Transition is a tax-exempt nonprofit organization with a 501(c)(3) status. This organization helps encourage transformation to individuals who are willing to break the bondage of abuse to find hope and take control of their lives. NWA Second Stage Transition seeks to strengthen linkages between clients and community-based resources to prevent the progression of abuse, addiction, and homelessness. NWA Second Stage Transition believes each person is created to be unique and valuable.

Mission

To educate, empower, provide safety, and opportunities to individuals and families who want to restore a balanced life and prevent the cycle of addiction, homelessness, and domestic violence.

Vision

A community where individuals and families are empowered, transformed, given hope, and held accountably.

Values

Professionalism • Confidentiality • Authenticity • Integrity • Inclusivity • Respect

Our Team

Our volunteers' staff, includes Licensed Professional Counselors (LPC), Licensed Drug & Alcohol Counselors (LDAC), Advanced Alcohol & Drug Counselors (AADC), and Clinical Supervisor for Drug and Alcohol, Pastoral Counseling (PC), Certified Parenting Instructor (CPI), Certified Anger Management Specialists (CAMS), and Domestic Violence Advocacy (DVA).
